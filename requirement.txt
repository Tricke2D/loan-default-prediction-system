CREDIT RISK SCORING – FEATURE DOCUMENTATION

This document explains each feature used in the risk scoring system, including its purpose, acceptable inputs, and validation rules.

1. person_age
Description:
Represents the applicant’s age at the time of loan application.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Age reflects financial maturity and stability.

Very young applicants tend to have higher risk due to limited financial history
Middle-aged applicants are generally more stable

Valid Input Examples:

21
30
45

Accepted Range:

Minimum: 18
Maximum: 100

Invalid Inputs:

Negative numbers
Zero
Extremely high values (e.g., 200+)
Non-numeric values (e.g., “thirty”)

2. person_income
Description:
Annual income of the applicant.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Income indicates repayment capacity.

Higher income → lower risk
Lower income → higher risk

Valid Input Examples:

50000000
120000000

Rules:

Must be a positive value
Should reflect realistic annual earnings

Invalid Inputs:

Negative values
Zero (generally unrealistic)
Text values (e.g., “50 million”)

3. person_home_ownership
Description:
Indicates the applicant’s housing status.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Home ownership reflects financial stability.

OWN → most stable
RENT → less stable

Allowed Values:

RENT
OWN
MORTGAGE

Invalid Inputs:

Any value outside the list above
Variations like “owned”, “renting”, etc.

4. person_emp_length
Description:
Number of years the applicant has been employed.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Employment length reflects job stability.

Longer employment → lower risk
Short employment → higher risk

Valid Input Examples:

1
5
10

Accepted Range:

0 to 50 years

Invalid Inputs:

Negative values
Text values

5. loan_intent
Description:
Purpose of the loan.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Different loan purposes carry different risk levels.

VENTURE → higher risk
PERSONAL → moderate
EDUCATION → generally lower

Allowed Values:

PERSONAL
EDUCATION
MEDICAL
VENTURE
HOMEIMPROVEMENT
DEBTCONSOLIDATION

Invalid Inputs:

Any value not listed above
Translated or informal terms

6. loan_grade
Description:
Credit grade assigned to the loan.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Direct indicator of creditworthiness.

A → lowest risk
G → highest risk

Allowed Values:

A, B, C, D, E, F, G

Invalid Inputs:

Any other letters or combinations (e.g., “AA”, “1”)

7. loan_amnt
Description:
Total loan amount requested.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Larger loans generally increase risk exposure.

Valid Input Examples:

5000000
20000000

Rules:

Must be a positive number

Invalid Inputs:

Negative values
Unrealistically large values

8. loan_int_rate
Description:
Interest rate of the loan (percentage).

Role in Risk Assessment:
Higher interest rates are often associated with higher-risk borrowers.

Valid Input Examples:

7.5
12.5
18.0

Accepted Range:

0 to 100

Invalid Inputs:

Negative values
Values above 100

9. loan_percent_income
Description:
Ratio of loan amount to annual income.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Measures financial burden.

Higher ratio → higher risk

Valid Input Examples:

0.1
0.3
0.5

Accepted Range:

0 to 1

Invalid Inputs:

Values greater than 1
Negative values

10. cb_person_default_on_file
Description:
Indicates whether the applicant has previously defaulted.

Role in Risk Assessment:
One of the strongest predictors of future default.

Y → significantly higher risk
N → lower risk

Allowed Values:

Y
N

Invalid Inputs:

“Yes”, “No”
Any other variation

11. cb_person_cred_hist_length
Description:
Length of the applicant’s credit history in years.

Role in Risk Assessment:
Longer credit history provides more reliable behavior patterns.

Longer history → lower risk

Valid Input Examples:

2
5
10

Invalid Inputs:

Negative values
Unrealistic values
